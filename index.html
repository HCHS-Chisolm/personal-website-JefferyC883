Lions

Lifespan: 15 - 16 years

Scientific Name: Panthera Leo

Conservation Status: Vunerable(Population Decreasing)

    Interesting Facts

  • Nearly all wild lions live in Africa, but one small population exists elsewhere
  • The magnificent manes on male lions tell a story
  • Lions can get their water from plants
  • They hunt during storms
  • Lions are the only cats who roar together

    Life of a Lion

  • Lions enjoy relaxing and lazing around.
  • They spend up to 21 hours each day resting and sleeping.
  • They conserve energy by resting during the day and being active at night when it's cooler.
  • Lions have terrific night vision.

    Raising Lions

  • Lion cubs start to walk at 10 to 15 days old
  • They grow baby teeth while they are very young
  • Baby lions are totally dependent on their mothers for food and protection during the first few months
  • The lionesses will keep her cubs hidden from other lions for up to two months

    Characteristics

  • Lions have strong, compact bodies and powerful forelegs, teeth and jaws for pulling down and killing prey
  • Their coats are yellow-gold, and adult males have shaggy manes that range in color from blond to reddish-brown to black
  • The length and color of a lion's mane is likely determined by age, genetics and hormones
